Aspect is a feature that specifies duration of the action in time,
whether the action has been completed etc. In Western Armenian some tenses are actually combinations of tense and aspect. 

Note, that some aspectual meanings in (secondary) compound tenses may be expressed at participles.

### <a name="Imp">`Imp`</a>: imperfect aspect

The action took / takes / will take some time span and there is no
information whether and when it was / will be completed.

#### Examples

* _նոյն համոզումը <b>ունէր</b>;_ “...”,
* _որոշապէս թուականը չեմ <b>յիշեր</b>;_ “...”

### <a name="Perf">`Perf`</a>: perfect aspect

The action has been / will have been completed. Since there is
emphasis on one point on the time scale (the point of completion),
this aspect does not work well with the present tense.

#### Examples

* _պուլկարական բանակը <b>թողուցի ու փախայ</b>;_ “...”,
* _շատ ուշ <b>ճանչցած</b> էր զայն;_ “...”,
* _այլեւս <b>մնացեր</b> էր հոն;_ “...”

### <a name="Prosp">`Prosp`</a>: prospective aspect

In general, prospective aspect can be described as relative future:
the action is/was/will be expected to take place at a moment that
follows the reference point; the reference point itself can be
in past, present or future.

In some contexts subjunctive and conditional forms may also indicate prospective aspect and signal an event that expected to take place. `Prosp` meaning prevails in conditional imperfect forms.

The future participle also indicate progressive aspect.

#### Examples

* _ալ անկարելի է որ հոս <b>մնամ</b>;_ “...”,
* _երբոր խօսքս <b>աւարտեմ</b>, պիտի թողուք որ <b>մեկնիմ</b>;_ “...”,
* _մեր <b>գիտնալիք</b> բանը չէ;_ “...”,
* _որ բան մը ունէր <b>ըսելիք</b>;_ “...”,
* _դեռ բաւական բաներ մը ունէին <b>պատրաստելիք</b>;_ “...”

### <a name="Prog">`Prog`</a>: progressive aspect

Action is in progress with respect to current or a reference time. The (AUX_)[] `կոր` indicate progressive aspect.

#### Examples

* _սարսափելի օրերու մէջ կ՚ապրինք <b>կոր</b>;_ “...”,
* _չեմ կրնար <b>կոր</b> ընկա&nbsp;_ “...”,
* _բայց ան կը սիրէ <b>կոր</b> քեզ;_ “...”,

### <a name="Dur">`Dur`</a>: durative aspect

A situation or action that has persisted over a period of time and still continues.

The subject participle indicate durative aspect.

#### Examples

* _խաւարի մէջ <b>ապրող</b> դիւային էակ մը;_ “...”,
* _ասդին անդին քուրջեր <b>հաւաքող</b> մարդիկ;_ “...”,
* _պատուհանէն <b>հոսող</b> առատ լոյսին տակ;_ “...”,

### <a name="Iter">`Iter`</a>: iterative

Denotes repeated action, with multiplicative or distributive meanings. Western Armenian has three main iterative markers _-ատ, -ըտ, -ուըռտ, -կլտ, -կոտ, -կռտ, -շտկ, -ոտ, -ուըտ, -ռտ, -տ, տկ_ or _root reduplication_.

Note, that in Western Armenian iterative is considered as a lexical feature of [verbs](VERB), thus they have morphologically related not iterative counterparts, but it is not a regular system and the two verbs are represented by different lemmas. We mark them as biaspectual.

#### Examples

* _թռչիլ&nbsp;_ “jump”, _<b>թռչկոտիլ</b>&nbsp;_ “jump several times”,
* _կտրել&nbsp;_ “cut”, _<b>կտրտել</b>&nbsp;_ “chop, cut in pieces”,
* _կռուիլ&nbsp;_ “to quarrel”, _<b>կռուըտիլ</b>&nbsp;_ “to quarrel”
