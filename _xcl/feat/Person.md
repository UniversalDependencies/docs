Person is a lexical feature of personal, possessive, and reflexive [pronouns](xcl-pos/PRON) / [determiners](xcl-pos/DET), and an inflectional feature of [auxiliaries](xcl-pos/AUX) / [verbs](xcl-pos/VERB). On verbs, the feature marks the person of the verb's subject. Person marked on verbs makes it unnecessary to always add a personal pronoun as subject and thus subjects are sometimes dropped (Classical Armenian is a pro-drop language).

### <a name="1">`1`</a>: first person

The first person refers to the speaker.

#### Examples

* Personal pronoun: ես/_es_ “I”
* Verb: ասեմ/_asem_ “I say”

### <a name="2">`2`</a>: second person

The second person refers to the addressee.

#### Examples

* Personal pronoun: դու/_dow_ “you”
* Verb: ասես/_ases_ “you say”

### <a name="3">`3`</a>: third person

The third person refers to participants that are neither
speakers nor addressees.

#### Examples

* Personal pronoun: իւր/_iwr_ “self”
* Verb: ասէ/_asē_ “(s)he says”
