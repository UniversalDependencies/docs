Sometimes there are multiple word forms for the same lemma and set of features.
The `Variant` feature helps distinguish alternate forms.

In Belarusian some [adjectives](be-pos/ADJ), [determiners](be-pos/DET), and, more regularly, participles may have a short form.
For the long forms, the `Variant` feature remains unspecified.

### <a name="Short">`Short`</a>: short form of adjectives

The short form is called _nominal form of adjective (краткая форма прилагательных),_
as opposed to the long form, which is _pronominal_ because it originated as
a combination of a nominal form and a personal pronoun clitic._

#### Examples

* _<b>рад</b>_ “glad”, _<b>повен</b>_ “full”, _<b>дзіўна</b>_ “beautiful”, _<b>упэўнена</b>_ “sure”, _<b>павінны</b>_ “have to”, _<b>актуальны</b>_ “relevant”,
  _<b>дазволена</b>_ “allowed”, _<b>удакладнены</b>_ “refined”, _<b>складзены</b>_.
* Long form equivalents: _рады_, _поўны_, _дзіўнае_, _упэўненае_, _павінныя_, _актуальныя_, _дазволенае_, _удакладненыя_, _складзеныя_.
