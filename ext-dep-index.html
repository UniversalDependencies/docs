Language-specific relations

In addition to the universal dependency taxonomy, it is desirable to recognize grammatical relations that are particular to one language or a small group of related languages. Such language-specific relations are necessary to accurately capture the genius of a particular language but will not involve concepts that generalize broadly. These language-specific relations should always be regarded as a subtype of an existing UD relation.

Labels of language-specific relations explictly encode the core UD relation that the language-specific relation is a subtype of, following the format universal:extension.

acl

  • acl:relcl: Danish, English, Finnish, French, German, Italian, Spanish, Swedish

advmod

  • advmod:emph: Ancient Greek, Arabic, Czech, Estonian, Latin, Portuguese, Tamil

auxpass

  • auxpass:reflex: Czech, Latin, Portuguese

cc

  • cc:preconj: English

ccomp

  • ccomp:nf: Finnish

compound

  • compound:prt: Danish, Dutch, English, Estonian, German, Swedish, Tamil

csubj

  • csubj:cop: Finnish

det

  • det:numgov: Czech
  • det:nummod: Czech, Dutch
  • det:poss: Italian
  • det:predet: English, Italian

nmod

  • nmod:gsubj: Finnish
  • nmod:gobj: Finnish
  • nmod:npmod: English
  • nmod:own: Finnish
  • nmod:poss: Danish, English, Finnish, French, Spanish, Swedish
  • nmod:loc: Danish
  • nmod:tmod: Danish, English
  • nmod:agent: Swedish

nsubj

  • nsubj:cop: Finnish

nummod

  • nummod:gov: Czech, Estonian
