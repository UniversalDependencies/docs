Degree of comparison is inflectional feature of some
[adjectives](cs-pos/ADJ) and [adverbs](cs-pos/ADV).

### <a name="Aug">`Aug`</a>: augmentative

The higher degree of manifestation of the characteristic (for adjectives) or the greater intensity of the action (for converbs) expressed by the head.

#### Examples
- _ɖimi -a <b>-l</b> =t //_ "It stinked a lot."

### <a name="Cmp">`Cmp`</a>: comparative, second degree

The quality of one object is compared to the same quality of another object.

#### Examples
- _ruːg -a <b>=ka</b> ti- ki //_ "it was calmer"

### <a name="Dim">`Dim`</a>: diminutive
Expression of the fact that something is smaller, it may be used to show affection.

#### Examples
- _<b>adaloː</b> na /_ "a small red thing"

### <a name="Equ">`Equ`</a>: Equative

The quality of one object is compared to the same quality of another object, and the result is that they are identical or similar (“as X as”). 

#### Examples
- _oːn suːr -n <b>=eːt</b> =hoːk / miskiːn ak -eːtiːt /_ "he became poor as before"
