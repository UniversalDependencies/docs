## ΑDV: adverb

### Definition

Adverbs are words that typically modify verbs for such categories as place, time, manner or quantity. They may also modify adjectives and other adverbs, as in πολύ έξυπνος, πολύ αργά respectively.

There is a closed subclass of pronominal adverbs that refer to circumstances in context, rather than naming them directly; they are distributed across the adverb categories defined above.

### Examples
-	locative adverbs: απέξω ‘outside’, εδώ ‘here’, εκεί ‘there’, πάνω ‘up’, κάτω ‘down’, δεξιά ‘right’, αριστερά ‘left’, κάπου ‘somewhere’, παντού ‘everywhere’, πουθενά ‘nowhere’, πού ‘where’
-	manner adverbs: ακριβώς ‘precisely’, γιατί ‘why’  (when it is on its own/it introduces direct questions), καλά ‘good/fine’, κατανάγκη ‘necessarily’, πώς ‘how’
-	adverbs ending in -όντας/ώντας: λέγοντας ‘by saying’, ρωτώντας ‘by asking’
-	temporal adverbs: αύριο ‘tomorrow’, κάποτε ‘sometime’, καταρχήν ‘first of all’, πάντα ‘always’, πέρσυ ‘last year’, πότε ‘when’, ποτέ ‘never’, σήμερα ‘today’, τότε ‘then’, τώρα ‘now’, χθες ‘yesterday’
-	quantity adverbs: άπαξ ‘once’, καθόλου ‘at all’, λίγο ‘a little’, μόνο ‘only’, (το) πολύ ‘very/max’, τόσο ‘so’
