### Definition

Adjectives are words that typically modify nouns and specify their properties or attributes. They may also function as predicates. These include the categories known as 區別詞 / _keoi1bit6ci4_ and 形容詞 / _jing4jung4ci4_.

- <b>好</b> 風景 / _<b>hou2</b> fung1ging2_ "good scenery"
- 天氣 好 <b>好</b> / _tin1hei3 hou2 <b>hou2</b>_ "The weather is good."

The adjective may by accompanied by the particle 嘅 / _ge3_ when functioning as a prenominal modifier (for either 區別詞 / _keoi1bit6ci4_ or 形容詞 / _jing4jung4ci4_), and often obligatorily when functioning as a predicate if it is a 區別詞 / _keoi1bit6ci4_. 

- <b>嚴重</b> 嘅 問題 / _<b>jim4zung6</b> ge3 man4tai4_ "serious problem"
- 呢 種 癌症 係 <b>慢性</b> 嘅 / _ni1 zung2 ngaam4zing3 hai6 <b>maan6sing3</b> ge3_ "This cancer spreads slowly (lit. is slow-acting)." 

Note that ordinal numerals such as 第一 / _dai6jat1_ "first" and 第三 / _dai6saam1_ "third" are to be treated as adjectives and tagged `ADJ` per [UD specifications](http://universaldependencies.org/u/pos/ADJ.html), even though they are traditionally classified as numerals in Chinese.

### Examples

- 形容詞 / _jing4jung4ci4_
  - 好 / _hou2_ "good", 靚 / _měilì_ "beautiful", 細 / _xiǎo_ "small", 老 / _lǎo_ "old"
- 區別詞 / _keoi1bit6ci4_
  - 女 / _neoi5_ "female", 慢性 / _maan6sing3_ "slow-acting", 親生 / _can1saang1_ "biological (kin relations)"
- Ordinal numbers
  - 第一 / _dai6jat1_ "first", 第三 / _dai6saam1_ "third", 第五十三 / _dai6ng2sap6saam1_ "fifty-third"
