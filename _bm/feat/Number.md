This feature typically applies to [nouns](bm-pos/NOUN) and other parts of speech that mark agreement with nouns, i.e [pronouns](bm-pos/PRON), [adjectives] (bm-POS/ADJ) and [determiners](bm-pos/DET).

In Bamana, the number marking for non-human is often optional, so the absence of a plural marker does not necessarily mean it is singular.

There are two plural markers: -w and -lù. -lù is less frequent and can be added only to animate nouns.

### <a name="Sing">`Sing`</a>: singular

In the treebank, `Number=Sing` is not used with nouns but it occurs with pronouns.

#### Examples

* _Cɛkɔrɔba n'<b>a</b> denw_ “old.man and <b>his</b> children”

### <a name="Plur">`Plur`</a>: plural

#### Examples

* _Cɛkɔrɔba n'a <b>denw</b>_ “old.man and his <b>children</b>”
